Project Proposal

Introduction/Background:

The goal of this project is to predict the instrument playing the music in a given sound bite. This is similar to Shazam, but instead of returning what song is playing, our program would return the instrument that is playing.

Problem Definition: 

Given an audio file of a single instrument playing, would it be possible to determine what instrument is playing the music heard in the file?

Methods:

  1. Identifying/Extracting Features
  1. MFCCs
  1. The sound sample is broken up into several frames. The result is a matrix of the number of coefficients by the number of frames.
  2. For optimal use with a neural net, use Principal Component Analysis and take the first 4 principal components. [2]
  1. Zero Crossing Rate
  1. The zero-crossing rate is the number of times a signal passes the value of zero in a time-domain interval. It is a measure of the dominance of low/high frequencies. [1]
  1. Spectrogram
  1. This gives us a frequency by time graph representing each of the audio files.
  1. Standard frequency: This is the standard unadjusted frequency (Hz).
  2. Mel Frequency: We convert the standard frequency to mel frequency to be more linear to our perception. [1]
  1. Chroma Energies for Each Pitch
  1. The entire frequency spectrum can be mapped logarithmically into 12 pitch classes, binned according to the musical octave. Chroma energy is the amount of spectral energy associated with each of the pitch classes. [1]
  1. Spectral Roll-off
  1. The spectral roll-off is a measure of the right-skewness of the frequency/power spectrum. The spectral roll-off is the frequency at which a specified percentage of the power spectrum is below. [1]
  1. Harmonic and Percussive Components
  1. In the time-domain representation of an audio signal, the amplitude of the signal can be decomposed into its harmonic and percussive amplitudes. [1]
  1. ML Algorithms
  1. Decision Trees
  1. Different instruments have different sonic properties. Using a decision tree to classify the instruments based on the features would be a method to identify an instrument in a sound file.
  1. Convolutional Neural Networks
  1. With convolutional neural networks (CNNs), it would be possible to take advantage of existing trends and make more complex connections based on these existing, simpler patterns. With the CNN approach, spectrograms, mel-spectrograms, or MFCCs could be used. [3]
  1. Quality Assurance
  1. Accuracy
  1. We can just count the number correct over the total samples tested on
  1. Confusion Matrix
  1. This will give us insight on which instruments are viewed similarly by the model.

There are various datasets to train the model on. Tensorflow’s NSynth dataset and IRMAS have annotated .wav files of individual instruments. Python’s Librosa and pyAudioAnalysis can extract audio features.

Potential Results:

We build a model that outputs the instrument playing in a sound clip of a single instrument. There would be a library of instruments that we have audio files on. The model would only select instruments from the given library of instruments.

Discussion:

One common issue is that certain instruments can sound similar at certain frequencies. Using spectrographs or other image data may be computationally expensive.
